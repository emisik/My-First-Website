Black Tea Processing

Raw Material

The raw material used to manufacture black tea is a greenleaf called camelia sinensisIt is harvested by plucking two leaves and a bud from the bush.The bud of camelia sinensis contains alot of flavour and it gives tea its characteristic taste and flavor

Manufacturing process

Reception

The raw material harvested from the field is taken through quality control checks to ensure that it meets the required standards.An ideal raw material used in the manufacture of black tea must have a bud with very soft two leaves.

Withering Process

The good quality raw material is then spread out in troughs to allow the withering process to begin.This process takes approximately about 12 to 18hours.The withering process allows the raw material to reduce its moisture. There are two types of withering:

  1. Chemical withering
  2. Physical withering

Chemical Withering

This is the process in which the chemical process inside the leaf takes place

Physical Withering

This is the process where the leaf looses its surface moisture.The required moisture in the leaf before a manufacturing process takes place is 68% to 70%

Maceration

This is the process where the greenleaf is crushed using a machine called rotor vane.The leaf is crushed to produce roughly cut leaf.

Cut Tear Curl

This is the process of finely cutting the macerated leaf into well rounded particles.This is done by using rollers with indentations.The rollers are designed in such a way that they can cut,tear and curl the leaf into fine particles

Oxidation

This is the process where the aroma and taste of the tea is created.There is a chemical process during this stage that happens and enables the tea to have its characteristic flavour.There is also a change of color in this stage from the characteristic green color from the greenleaf to a coppery brown color.

Drying

This is the stage where the oxidized dhool is dried through the removal of moisture from 65% to between 2.0% to 3.0%.The color of the dhool then changes from coppery brown to black.This is done by using dryers Dryers differ from one design to another but their functionality is the same.This process is very important in ensuring that microbes do not thrive in the tea.

Sortation and Grading

The fully dried tea is then passed through rollers that remove any excess fibre from the tea.The tea is then passed through meshes of different sizes to enable the tea to be separated based on size.
